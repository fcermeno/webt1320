TRADITIONS IN VENEZUELAN CULTURE

Family Traditions

Venezuela remains a patriarchal society and its family traditions closely follow. Men are expected to be the bread winners, while women are expected to stay home and rear children. Family ties, including extended family ties, are important in Venezuela, evidenced by most members living in close proximity. Although these traditions and kinship ties are the cornerstone of society in Venezuela, you will find that they are much more relaxed in Caracas. Western influence has led to women working outside of the home, acquiring a college degree and breaking away from traditional gender roles.

Christmas Traditions

Caracas is Venezuela's hub of culture, and Christmas traditions there fall in with the prevailing beliefs of the country. Most Venezuelans are Catholic and celebrate the birth of Jesus on December 25. In Caracas, and throughout the country, festivities begin on December 16 with the preparation of the Hallacas, a christmas food, and mass church services each morning. The final service, Misa de Aguinaldo or Misa de Gallo, occurs at midnight on Noche Buena, or Christmas Eve. Traditionally, Venezuelans erect a nativity scene, but Christmas trees also are very common. After a big family gathering and a traditional meal on Noche Buena.

Food Traditions

Try arepa, a fried corn pancake that is found stuffed with different types of meat, seafood and vegetables. Other fried, filled foods popular in Caracas include empanadas, turnovers made from corn meal, and cachitos, croissants often filled with ham and cheese. If you find yourself in Caracas during the Christmas season, try hallaca, a traditional dish consisting of a corn batter containing various types of meat, vegetables, herbs, spices and raisins, and wrapped and steamed in banana leaves.
